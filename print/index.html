Act 3: Roadmap for a great presentation

  1. Think about intended Goal, Qualities and Aspects
  2. Budget time, then make the presentation
  3. Reflect, make alterations if needed (don't wanna forget to add in your JavaScript jokes!)
  4. Prepare before the presentation
  5. Present!
  6. Reflect again

A few helpful considerations/questions:

  • Who is your audience? What is their familiarity with your subject?
  • What's the takeaway?
  • Do you want/expect people to refer back to the slide deck afterwards?
  • Will the presentation be recorded?
  • Can you actually do one of these fancy three-words-per-slide decks?
  • Are there constraints to the tone? (e.g. business formal)
  • How high of a bar is expected?
  • Do you want to make it awesome? Do you have the time to do so?

Appendix I: Common mistakes & Hacks

Common mistakes

  • No slide numbers
  • Not introducing new topics properly
  • Not guiding user attention properly
    • Too many transitions / too few transitions / too flashy transitions
  • Nervosity causing bad breathing cadence and incorrect speaking tempo
    • Solution: calm down, drink some water!
    • Clear your mind before you start; do not commit to a deep conversation just before starting!
  • Not specifying whether it's okay to interrupt during the presentation or if you'd prefer questions at the end
  • Sloppy slide template usage (e.g. forgetting to update the year in the copyright notice, sample text)

Some more common mistakes

  • Unreadable text (incorrect text sizes, bad contrast, usage of color to convey important information that canâ€™t be seen by the colorblind)
